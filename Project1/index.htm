Project 1: Website Critique

    www.jamilin.com

  1. What was your first impression of this site?

    2. My first thoughts opening the website were about how dated it looks. The vibrant myriad of colors along with the font
    and styles do not help with readability. The resolution of the photos looks old and unattractive.
    It doesn't feel professional.

  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)

    3. The flow of information feels out of order. There are links towards the middle of the page, but none at the top.
    The volume of information around the links feels cluttered and confusing.

  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?

    4. The structure of the main page does not make sense to me. The layout is inconsistent in spacing.
    I don't agree with the choice of the site heading and with how much space is used. The hierarchy of
    information is not well put together, making the page confusing to navigate. The bottom half of the
    home page looks like it should be its own page. The contact links and other links are out of place.

  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?

    5. The layout is detrimental to any viewers experience. The spacing is off-putting. There are too
    many inconsistencies in the sizes of graphics and headings. The resolution of the photos look dated.
    The use of the same photo or similar photos is irrelevant to the information.

  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?

    6. The home page loaded reasonably fast on desktop. The attributes looked like they loaded in different speeds though.
    Loading the page on mobile was significantly slower though.

  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?

    7. The website uses a variety of colors and fonts, which intended to reflect the vibrancy of the content.
    However, there were too many inconsistencies with fonts and no unified color palette, which would have
    helped with readability.

  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?

    8. There was no navigation bar available on the home page, but there were on the other websites linked
    with this one. Overall, the navigation on this website makes it difficult to find any desired content.

  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?

    9. The color scheme across the home page and its other linked website pages were inconsistent. One was green,
    another was blue, and another one orange.

  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?

    10. There is no responsive design on desktop. Changing window sizes does not affect content layout.
    On mobile, the website asks you to use landscape mode view the website. The content does not adapt
    well to the window size on mobile.

  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

    11. The vibrancy from the colors and the many smiling photos speaks of the website owner's personality.
    You can tell that she loves what she does, and she takes more pride in her services than having
    a pretty website.
